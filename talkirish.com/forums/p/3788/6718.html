    Re: Ard Mhacha or Ard Macha?

    Reply | Contact

    Possibly because some sources referencing older books don't think to bring the lenition (the 'buailte') on the 'M' into modern fonts with a 'h'.  Most sources explain Ard Mhacha as 'the height/hill of Macha', Macha being a presumed Celtic goddess, the one who - according to some tales - cursed the men of Ulster to suffer her labour pains in the hour of their greatest need, leaving Cúchulainn to defend the province in the Táin story.  In Wikipedia, Eamhain Mhacha, one of the principal prehistoric sites in Ulster, is listed as "known in Old Irish as Eṁaın Ṁacha" (note the dots on the Ms!). The same grammatical rules would have put a dot on the Macha of Armagh, in current spelling making it Mhacha.
