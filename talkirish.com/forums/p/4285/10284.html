    Haigh, JD.

    I like your website concept, it's very cute!  I also like the very basic stories with Farg.  I have several things to offer, particularly as pertains to Irish grammar.

    1.  You seem to be doing a word-for-word translation of English to Irish, which pays no attention to Irish sentence structure.  Irish sentences are constructed in VSO order -- that is, Verb, Subject, Object.  In English, we are used to having the verb separate the subject and object, but Irish is quite different.  So, for example, frame 1 of your story about the dam has "An Tollamh Tinklebottom chinneann chun plandaí a buama ar an damba sna cnoic thuas Fargs teach."  The English version is, "Professor Tinklebottom decides to plant a bomb on the dam in the hills above Farg's house."  Assuming your verb "chinnean" is correct for "decides", the sentence should place the verb first, ahead of the subject.  But in looking up "cinn" my online dictionary shows it as an intransitive verb, and it doesn't show a standard conjugation.  It shows "cinneadh air" as "making up one's mind."  I might suggest using the verb "beartaigh" which means "to contrive, scheme, or wield", and the third person singular would be "beartaíonn".  So your sentence would start out "Beartaíonn an Tollamh Tinklebottom ...."

    2.  "Plant" as a verb is "plandaigh", and I think "plandáil" is the verbal noun form, so change "plandaí" to "plandáil". and restructure that part of the sentence:  "Beartaíonn an Tollamh Tinkelbottom ar plandáil buama ar an damba sna cnoic thuas ...."  Please note the elimination of the indefinite article "a" before "buama" as there is no indefinite article in Irish, and the particle "a" inserted there (which can be used a number of ways) would be incredibly confusing.

    3.  The name of the person who possesses the house "teach" should follow rather than precede the word, and the definite article can be added:  "Beartaíonn an Tollamh Tinkelbottom ar plandáil buama ar an damba sna cnoic thuas an teach Farg."

    There may be some better construct for this, but I'm still quite a learner myself....

    Each of your other frames have similar issues.  Obviously some words you were unable to find a good translation or alternative for, such as "whizzing" in frame 2.  (For that one, I might suggest "scinneadh thart" which means "to flash by or past".

    In Frame 3, you have a like problem with the verb not being first, and at the end of the sentence you should have "damba nua síos sruth" as "new" applies to the dam.

    In Frame 4, again, verb first, and an Irish one, not English:  "Pléascann an buama!"  "The bomb explodes!"  The construct of the next sentence looks pretty strange, and it needs going over in more detail; the last sentence should be, "Tá an Tollamh Tinklebottom bactha arís!"  "Professor Tinklebottom is frustrated/thwarted again!"

    Also in Frame 4, on the art work, in the English version, you show Tinklebottom yelling, "NO!"  In the Irish artwork he yells, "AON!" "Aon" is Irish for "one" as in the number, and is not what he would yell to show his frustration.  "Ní hea!" is the retort I have seen used in several places, "Not that!"

    On the same art, you use the English "BOOM!" in both places for the bomb's explosion.  This may be ok, but if you want to substitute an Irish term on the Irish version, I would suggest "BúIREACH!"  (With a capital U-fada of course; I can't locate my shortcut for the capitals with fadas on them....)

    I'm not sure where you are with your study of Irish, but it is good to ask for help with a project like this.  I still consider myself a learner, but I'm happy to pass on what I'm comfortable with.  Sometimes I go out a little on a limb, and usually seánó comes to my rescue!

    Good luck!

    Hi, JD.

    I'm happy to respond to posts on the forum, and help with what I know, but I'm far from fluent, and wouldn't want to make a commitment to something I'm not so sure of.
