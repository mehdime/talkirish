    Ok, I thouht I would set up a thread where poepl could share grammar tips questions etc. I'll get it started:

    An Forainm Réamhfhochlach (personalized prepostions, that's not the translation that's just what they are)

    Ag  =  At     As = out of    Chuig = towards   Do = to/for  Ar = on   Trí = through  De = off 

    agam         asam             chugam               dom           orm        tríom             díom        me

    agat           asat              chugat                 duit            ort          tríot              díot          you

    aige           as                 chuige                 dó              air          tríd               de            him

    aici            aisti               chuici                 di               uirthi      tríthi              di             her

    againn       asainn            chugainn            dúinn          orainn     trinn             dinn          us

    agaibh       asaibh            chugaibh            daoibh        oraibh     tríbh             díbh          you pl.

    acu           astu               chucu                 dóibh         orthu       tríothu          díobh        them

     

    This list is not comprehensive and I apologise for the poor layout. I hope it is simple enough for example to say "towards him" look at chuig (towards) and then move down until it coincides with him and you get "chuige" (towards him). PS: on a side note chuig is only used when describing people you can say I was going towards someone with chuige but not I was going towards somewhere. I hope that that was useful. Please add any grammar tips etc. that you mght have.

    Maith thú, a Leipreacháin! This is very useful stuff. The whole basis of the Irish language is the prepositions, so the sooner you learn all these forms, the better. I'm sure you will all realise this anyway, but these forms always have echoes of the pronouns they contain. So all the mé forms have m at the end, all the tú forms have t at the end, all the sí forms have a short i at the end, all the forms for us have -inn at the end (from sinn), all the sibh forms have -ibh at the end. The third person forms are a bit more of a mixed bag, though the third person plural (they) forms all end in -u.

    As for the advice about chuig and places, you are right in practical terms. You wouldn't say Bhí cathair ann agus chuaigh muid (or chuamar) chuici, because you would be thinking of a woman or girl rather than a city. However, you could say "Chuaigh siad chuig sráidbhaile a raibh droichead galánta ann." (=They went to a village with a beautiful bridge.) In other words, you can use chuig with places, but not the inflected prepositional pronoun forms you have laid out in a table above.

    Haigh, a Chiaráin. Cuid de na forainmneacha réamhfhoclacha seo, bíonn siad míle uair níos coitianta ná an chuid eile. Mar shampla, baintear an-úsáid as agam, orm agus liom. Ní bhaintear an-úsáid as tríom nó tharam agus is doiligh agam smaoineamh ar shamplaí.

    Tháinig mé air seo ar an idirlíon:

    Chuireas méara i mó chluasa mar bhí an ceol brónach sin ag dul tríom, ag dul go smior. (I put fingers in my ears because the sad music was going through me, going to the marrow.)

    Agus seo an úsáid is coitianta a bhaintear as tharam:

    Níor lig mé tharam an deis leis an obair a chríochnú. (I didn't let the opportunity past me to finish the work)

    Maidir leis an fhocal asam, seo roinnt samplaí. 

    Bhí siad ag spochadh asam. (They were mocking me)

    Bhain an trup sin geit asam. (That noise took a start out of me)

    Tá mo thuismitheoirí bródúil asam. (My parents are proud of me)

    Tá súil agam go bhfuil sé seo ina  chuidiú!
