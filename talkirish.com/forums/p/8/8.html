    I've done a quick Google search on the Coligny Calendar, and found this post http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coligny_calendar at wikipedia. The calendar was found in Coligny, France (which was known as Gaul to the Romans at the time). It dates back to either the first or second century AD. The calendar's makers would have been Celts.

    I've no idea how to pronounce these words - I think we'd need an expert in Ancient Celtic languages to help with that. But I found this site http://www.roman-britain.org/coligny.htm which has translations of the months but I don't know how accurate they are. Ogronios is translated as Time of Ice, Simivisionios is Time of Brightness.

     Hope this is interesting!
