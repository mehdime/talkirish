    Hi Dóiteáin, Sorry not to have got back to you sooner! I didn't notice your post until now. Anyway, in answer to your question, that translation is correct but a bit odd in terms of word order. "It's many a ragged colt that made a powerful horse." 

    You pronounce it like this:

    ISS MINNIK A RINN(YA) BROMMAKH GIBBLAKH COPPULL COMMASSAKH

    Hi SK, Welcome to the Forum! You seem to have found the right words there. As you say, it is Naomh Bríd na hÉireann. (NAOMH BRÍD NA HÉIREANN).

    There are several ways to pronounce it, depending on dialect, but the least confusing way is: NEEV BREEJ NA HAIRON. NEEV is fairly self-explanatory, I think. BREEJ is also pretty easy to understand - it sounds like a bridge in Mexico ( - when it's not a puente, that is!!) NA presents no problems, and HAIRON is like "keep your HAIR ON"!!!

    Enjoy the confirmation! S.
