    Haigh, Bama!

    You could say, "Tá mé ag dul a leaba" I'm going to my bed, but it appears the Irish idiom leans more toward saying, "Tá mé ag dul a luí" I am going to lay down.  The latter phrase is active rather than passive, which may be why it's preferred, but the English phrase "I'm going to bed" does literally mean "I'm going to get in bed."  The phrase "Tá mé ag dul a leaba" could be translated simply as, I'm going to my bed but would not necessarily indicate that you intended to lay down on it....

    Of course, if I'm off base here, hopefully someone will chime in to correct me!

    In Irish we generally say 'I'm going to sleep' rather than '...to bed'

     

    Tá mé ag dul a choladh.

     

    "Tá mé" can become "Táim" for greater fluency.

     

     

    Táim ag dul a choladh.

    Tá mé ag dul a luí (literally, I'm going to lie)

    Tá mé ag dul a chodladh (literally, I'm going to sleep)

    Tá mé ag dul don leaba (literally, I'm going to the bed)

    Tá mé ag dul chun soip ('sop' is a bed made of straw...like the English phrase 'hitting the hay')
