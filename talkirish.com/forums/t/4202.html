    For Ever and Ever (Amen)

    Reply | Contact

    Hi, We have had an enquiry from a lady in England whose grandmother was raised in Wicklow. They used to say a prayer each night, ending with the phrase 'for ever and ever', so this lady would like to find out how to write this phrase and how to pronounce it. There are several ways of saying it, the most common being ó shaol go saol (áiméan). The word saol is one of those strange hard-to-translate expressions which has no exact equivalent in English. It means something like life or world and is ultimately derived from the Etruscan language, which gave the Latin language the word saeculum. This was then transformed into saoghal and thence saol in Modern Irish. The phrase "ó shaol go saol" means something like "from world to world" or "from life to life" or "from age to age". It is pronounced roughly as "oh heal go seal."

    Amen is pretty much the same in all languages - in Irish it's spelled áiméan.

    Hope we aren't too late with this and a big Breithlá sona to your grandmother! :-)

    The Our father ends with 'for ever and ever'

    The Lord's Prayer

    (traditional)

     

    Our Father, which art in heaven,
    Hallowed be thy Name.
    Thy Kingdom come.
    Thy will be done in earth,
    As it is in heaven.
    Give us this day our daily bread.
    And forgive us our trespasses,
    As we forgive them that trespass against us.


    And lead us not into temptation,
    But deliver us from evil.
    For thine is the kingdom,

    The power, and the glory,

    For ever and ever.

    Amen.

    Ár nAthair
    Ár nAthair, atá ar neamh: go naofar d'ainm.
    Go dtaga do ríocht.
    Go ndéantar do thoil ar talamh
    mar a dhéantar ar neamh.
    Ár n-arán laethúil tabhair dúinn inniu,
    agus maith dúinn ár bhfiacha,
    mar a mhaithimid dár bhféichiúnaithe féin.
    Agus ná lig sinn i gcathú,
    ach saor sinn ó olc.
    [Óir is leatsa an Ríocht agus an Chumhacht
    agus an Ghlóir, trí shaol na saol.]
    Áiméan
