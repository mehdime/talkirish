    Hello.

    I have a former work associate who has this name, but spells it quite differently, and pronounces it with your first option.

    But I question whether this is an Irish name, or certainly not the Irish spelling in any case, as it does not follow the broad/slender vowel convention, and also has an "h" after the "n", and I don't think that combination occurs in Irish.  I could be wrong, but don't remember seeing it anywhere.

    Conventional vowel construction in Irish is designed to give the consonants their broad or slender quality.  The broad vowels are a, o, and u; the slender are e and i.  The rule is, when you have a broad vowel on one side of a consonant, you need a broad vowel on the other side to balance it for pronunciation.  That is clearly not the case in the spelling you have given.  To make it fit the rule, you would have to insert either an e or an i after the initial A, or insert an o, u or a before the i.  The first option would be most likely, probably with an i being inserted there.  Of course, that would make it conform to the spelling conventions in Irish, but wouldn't necessarily make it an Irish name!

    A quick search of Yahoo! brings up a number of sites on Irish names.  I like this one in particular:  http://www.namenerds.com/irish/lists.html

    If you are looking for a truly Irish name, that might be a good place to start.
